I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the [Technical University of Munich](https://www.tum.de/), in the [Professorship for Business Analytics & Intelligent Systems](https://www.bais.mgt.tum.de/) led by [Prof. Maximilian Schiffer](https://www.bais.mgt.tum.de/en/team/maximilian-schiffer/). My research focuses on learning-based control for robotic and autonomous systems, with an emphasis on offline and hierarchical reinforcement learning, and on structured policies that integrate classical control and optimization for scalability, robustness, and feasibility under operational constraints.

I completed my Ph.D. in Machine Learning and Optimization at the [Technical University of Denmark](https://www.dtu.dk/) in 2024, advised by [Filipe Rodrigues](https://fprodrigues.com/), [Francisco C. Pereira](https://camara.scripts.mit.edu/home/), and [Daniele Gammelli](https://danielegammelli.github.io/). Prior to joining TUM, I held a postdoctoral position at DTU in the [Machine Learning for Smart Mobility Lab](https://mlsm.man.dtu.dk/), and was a visiting researcher at the [Autonomous Systems Lab](https://stanfordasl.github.io/) at Stanford University, where I worked with [Prof. Marco Pavone](https://web.stanford.edu/~pavone/) on integrating deep reinforcement learning with optimization and control. I received my M.Sc. (with honors) in Electrical Power Engineering and Operations Research from [RWTH Aachen University](https://www.rwth-aachen.de/).

My collaborations span Stanford, MIT, DTU, TUM, and Google DeepMind. Most recently, I have been working on offline RL for combinatorial action spaces, fast-and-slow planning architectures for control, and structured RL solutions for industrial supply-chain problems in collaboration with [SAP](https://www.sap.com/).
